Fixate

Author: Fixate  |  Source: http://fixate.it/

How far can you push SVG? This site shows one way to embrace SVG for responsive web design. There’s no shortage of ornamentation, yet the message is clear: This is an agency of creative illustration. If you’re a client looking for conventional ideas and staid product design, look elsewhere.

What They Had To Say
Cassandra Zamparini
Partner at Fixate

We greatly underestimated the amount of work that would go into the illustrations - not just one animal per header, but one for mobile, tablet, and desktop - they're all different. After that, it was a challenge to get a mixture of inline and image SVG's to scale proportionately across devices, whilst providing fallbacks for the old IEs.

We're really happy with the way it came out. It's funny to see people say our design is simple and minimal. Those beasts were beasts!

Cassandra Zamparini
Partner at Fixate

It's important to us that our web capabilities, qualities, and values are reflected on our own site — we have to practise what we preach! We've had visitors with screens from 5760x1080 right down to 0x0 (yes... you read that correctly. Either someone has a very limited view of the internet, or Google Analytics is playing games with us), so it's great to be able to cater to everyone.

Also, we're terrified of horizontal scroll bars on fixed width sites. They give us the heebie-jeebies!

Cassandra Zamparini
Partner at Fixate

We wanted to try something that we'd never seen before without having to go too crazy on functionality and features. There are a lot of great sites out there doing insane and awesome things with JS and CSS, so competing in that sphere, while not being too 'trendy' or gimmicky is difficult. Exploring other avenues of our skills, such as illustration, made sense to us.

Deviating from current trends seemed to be the best direction to take, although we were unsure of how our design would be received. It's worked out well — we're really happy with how people have reacted.
